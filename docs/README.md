## Process Overview

OctoAcme's project management lifecycle moves through five key phases: **initiation → planning → execution → release → retrospective**. Each phase has defined artifacts, checklists, and decision gates to keep delivery outcome-focused and consistent across teams.

### Initiation
Work begins by validating the business need and aligning stakeholders. Teams produce a lightweight Project One-pager capturing the problem statement, SMART goals, success metrics, high-level timeline, and initial risks. A go/no-go decision gate confirms that success metrics are clear and team availability is confirmed before moving into planning.

### Planning
Approved projects are translated into a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria, effort estimates, dependencies, and a documented Definition of Done. Milestones and a release plan are established so that teams can execute in small, shippable increments with measurable outcomes.

### Execution & Tracking
Delivery is managed through project boards with explicit status columns (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done). Teams hold frequent standups to surface blockers and run weekly delivery syncs to review progress, risk, and scope. Stakeholder updates are shared on a regular cadence, and a single communication template ensures consistency. Roles are clearly defined — **Product Managers** own prioritization and outcomes, **Project Managers** coordinate schedules and risk, **Developers** implement and test features, and **QA contributors** validate acceptance criteria.

### Risk Management & Communication
Risk management is continuous throughout the lifecycle. Risks are logged with impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation in a shared Risk Register, reviewed at weekly syncs, and escalated through a defined path from team-level triage up to sponsor-level escalation when business impact is significant. Standardized communication templates for weekly status reports and incident updates reinforce a single source of truth.

### Release & Deployment
Before a release, teams confirm that all CI checks and security scans pass, release notes are prepared, a rollback plan exists, and post-deploy verification steps are documented. After deployment, a release health check validates that the feature or service is performing as expected.

### Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
After each sprint, release, or incident, the team holds a retrospective to capture what went well, what could be improved, and what action items should be owned and tracked. Lessons learned are converted into concrete improvements, reinforcing a continuous improvement loop across teams.
